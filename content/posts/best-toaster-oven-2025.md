## Toaster Oven vs. Air Fryer vs. Full Oven

Before recommending specific models, it's worth clarifying the landscape:

**Traditional toaster oven**: Uses radiant heating elements. Great for toast, baking, broiling, reheating pizza. Limited airflow means slower cooking and less crispiness than an air fryer.

**Air fryer toaster oven**: Adds a convection fan that circulates hot air rapidly — producing crispy results similar to deep frying with little to no oil. This is the category that has exploded in popularity.

**Full oven**: Higher capacity, more even heating for large items, essential for big baking projects. Uses 3–4x more energy than a toaster oven and takes much longer to preheat.

For most people, a modern air fryer toaster oven can replace both a countertop air fryer and a traditional toaster oven, and handle 80% of what a full oven does — in less time, with less energy.

## What to Look for in a Toaster Oven

**Interior size**: Measure the interior (not exterior) dimensions. Can it fit a 12-inch pizza? Two slices of bread? A sheet pan for cookies?

**Convection fan**: Essential for air frying and even baking. Single-speed fans are adequate; dual-speed (like Breville) gives more control.

**Temperature range and accuracy**: A wide range (100°F–500°F or higher) and accurate temperature control matter for baking and dehydrating.

**Number of functions**: More functions aren't always better — consider which ones you'll actually use. Toast, bake, broil, and air fry cover 90% of use cases.

**Ease of cleaning**: Removable crumb trays, non-stick interiors, and dishwasher-safe accessories save significant time. The Breville's interior is easy to wipe down. Some competitors trap grease in hard-to-reach areas.

**Build quality**: Look for stainless steel construction and a solid door hinge. Cheap plastic handles and wobbly doors indicate poor construction.

## Energy Efficiency

Toaster ovens use 1,200–1,800W, compared to a conventional oven's 2,000–5,000W. For meals that fit in a toaster oven, the energy savings are significant — especially for preheating (2–5 minutes vs. 15–25 minutes for a full oven).

## Cleaning Tips

- **Clean after every use**: Wipe the interior with a damp cloth while still warm (not hot)
- **Remove crumb tray after every use**: Crumbs are a fire hazard if allowed to accumulate
- **Non-stick interior**: Use a non-abrasive cloth to preserve the coating
- **Glass door**: Use glass cleaner or vinegar solution weekly
