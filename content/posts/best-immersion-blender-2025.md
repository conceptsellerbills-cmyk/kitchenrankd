An immersion blender (also called a stick blender or hand blender) is one of the most practical tools in a modern kitchen. It lets you blend soups directly in the pot, puree sauces without transferring to a countertop blender, make quick smoothies, emulsify dressings, and whip cream. With over 500,000 monthly searches, "best immersion blender" is a popular search for good reason — these compact tools punch well above their weight.

This guide covers the best immersion blenders in 2025 for every need and budget.

## Why an Immersion Blender vs. a Countertop Blender?

**Immersion blender advantages:**
- Blend directly in a pot, bowl, or container — no transferring hot liquids
- Compact — takes minimal storage space
- Easy to clean — usually just rinse the blade attachment under water
- Faster for small quantities
- No splashing hot liquid risk (common when transferring to a countertop blender)

**Countertop blender advantages:**
- More power for crushing ice and frozen fruit
- Better for large batches
- Produces smoother results for fibrous ingredients

For soups, sauces, dressings, baby food, and whipped cream, an immersion blender is often the superior tool. For smoothies with ice or frozen fruit, a countertop blender is still better.

## Key Specs to Look For

**Motor power**: Measured in watts. More watts = more power for tough ingredients. Range: 150W (basic) to 1,000W+ (professional). For most home use, 200–400W is sufficient.

**Blade length/depth**: A longer blending arm reaches deeper pots. Most consumer models: 6–8 inches.

**Speed settings**: Variable speed or multiple settings allow more control. A single speed is limiting.

**Attachments**: Many models come with a whisk attachment (for whipped cream, eggs), a food processor bowl, and a blending cup. The more useful attachments, the better the overall value.

**Detachable shaft**: Makes cleaning easier. Look for this — it's nearly standard on good models.

**Ergonomics**: A comfortable, non-slip grip matters when using one-handed. Buttons should be reachable without shifting your hand.

## Immersion Blender Uses and Tips

**Perfect soups every time**: For creamy soups (butternut squash, tomato, potato leek), immersion blend directly in the pot. Hold the blender near the bottom and move it slowly upward in a circular motion. The bell guard prevents splatter.

**Homemade mayonnaise in 30 seconds**: Add all ingredients to a tall narrow cup, lower the immersion blender to the bottom (over the egg yolk), turn on at low speed, slowly pull upward over 30 seconds. The emulsification is instant and perfect.

**Whipped cream**: Use the whisk attachment in a tall, narrow cup or bowl. Cream whips in 60–90 seconds.

**Emulsified dressings**: Blend oil and vinegar directly in a jar. The emulsification stays stable far longer than whisked dressings.

**Baby food**: Steam vegetables, then immersion blend directly in the pot with a little cooking water. Smooth, fresh, and free of additives.

**Safety**: Never blend with the blender fully out of liquid — the blade is exposed and will spray. Always submerge before turning on. Blend away from clothing.

## Cleaning and Maintenance

- **Immediate cleaning**: Rinse under hot water immediately after use while food hasn't dried. 90% of cleaning is this easy step.
- **Dishwasher**: Most blending shafts are dishwasher-safe. Check your model's manual.
- **Stuck-on food**: Fill a cup with warm water and a drop of dish soap. Run the blender in the water for 10 seconds.
